FEMA & Legal NRI Guidelines

Clear answers to common legal, tax, and repatriation questions for overseas Indian citizens.

Yes! Under Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and FEMA guidelines, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs) holding valid passports can freely purchase residential plots, villas, and commercial property in India without prior RBI approval. (Note: Agricultural land and plantations are restricted).

Payments must be made through normal banking channels via inward remittance from overseas bank accounts, or through NRE (Non-Resident External) or NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) account balances in India. Cash payments are strictly prohibited.

You can execute a Power of Attorney (PoA) in favor of a trusted family member or legal representative in India. The PoA document must be signed in your country of residence, attested by the Indian Consulate/Embassy (or apostilled), and registered locally in Bangalore before the property deed execution.

Yes! Under FEMA guidelines, NRIs can repatriate sale proceeds up to USD 1 Million per financial year out of NRO account balances, subject to payment of applicable capital gains tax in India and submission of Form 15CA/15CB certified by a Chartered Accountant.
